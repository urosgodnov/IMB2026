# Block 5 Bonus — A Research Cockpit: NotebookLM

## Goal
Meet a different kind of AI surface. Antigravity (Block 5) was an agent that *acts*
on files. NotebookLM is the opposite: a free, Gemini-powered tool that *reads your
documents* and answers from them — with citations — and can turn them into a study
guide or a podcast. You will feed it this workshop's own handouts and watch it
become an instant study buddy. No API key, no payment — just a Google account.

## Type this
You will not type code here — this is a web tool. Follow the clicks.

1. Open [notebooklm.google.com](https://notebooklm.google.com) and sign in with the
   **same Google account** you used for Antigravity (free, no payment).
2. Click **Create** (or **New notebook**).
3. **Add sources**: click **Add** and upload the workshop handout PDFs your
   facilitator shared (the `pdf/` folder — e.g. `b2-loop-and-goal.pdf`,
   `b3-efforts-and-tokens.pdf`, `b4-mcp-and-github.pdf`, and `README.pdf`). You can
   also paste a link or text as a source.
4. Wait a few seconds for NotebookLM to read the sources, then ask a grounded
   question in the chat box:
   `What is the Read-Plan-Edit-Verify loop, and why does it matter?`
5. Click a **citation chip** in the answer — it jumps to the exact spot in the
   source it used.
6. In the **Studio** panel, click **Study Guide** to generate one from the sources.
7. Still in **Studio**, click **Audio Overview** and **Generate**. After a minute
   or two you get a short podcast — two AI hosts discussing *this workshop*. Press
   play.

## You should see
- Your uploaded PDFs listed as **Sources** on the left.
- A chat answer that explains the loop **and shows citation chips** pointing back
  into the handouts — not a generic answer from the open web.
- A **Study Guide** with key terms and review questions drawn from your sources.
- An **Audio Overview**: a ~5-minute, two-host audio "deep dive" summarising the
  workshop you just did.

## If it looks wrong
- **Can't sign in / no notebook appears**: confirm you are logged into a personal
  Google account (the same one from Block 5). NotebookLM is free but requires a
  Google sign-in.
- **Upload rejected or "limit reached"**: the free tier caps how many sources and
  how much you can generate per day. Upload fewer PDFs (B2 and B3 are enough), or
  switch to the facilitator's screen.
- **Audio Overview is slow or queued**: generation takes a minute or two and can
  queue at busy times — keep exploring the chat while it finishes, or watch the
  facilitator's pre-generated one.
- **Answer has no citations**: you likely asked before the sources finished
  loading, or asked something the sources don't cover. Re-ask once the sources show
  as ready, and keep the question about the handout content.
